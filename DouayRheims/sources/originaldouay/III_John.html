He commendeth Gaius, for continuing in the truth, & for susteining or succouring true Preachers, 9 noting Diotrepes for the contrarie, and praising Demetrius.
1. THE Seniour to Gaius the Dearest, whom I loue in truth. 2. My Deerest, concerning al things I make my praier that thou proceed prosperously, and fare wel, as thy soule doth prosperously. 3. I had many things to write vnto thee: but I would not by inke and penne write to thee. 14. But I hope forth-with to see thee, and we wil speake mouth to mouth. Peace be to thee.
